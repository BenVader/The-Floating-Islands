THE FLOATING ISLES CHAPTER SYNOPSIS 

Prologue  

Our story is set in the world of The Floating Isles, where mysterious forces have raised up the land, and hold it suspended in the air. From small archipelagos of little more than rocks and boulders, to majestic continental shelves; these lands hang at different heights above the world ocean, that broils deep below with storms and mist.  

These Floating Isles for the most part move glacially slowly, in an intricate dance that when measured at the human level can be barely perceptible, punctuated only by a few rogue islands that soar across the sky to their own inscrutable beat. Legends speak of a cycle of periodical chaos, where the sedate steps are interrupted by unpredictable upheaval, when even the largest and most populated continents are not safe from quakes and collisions. The periods are called by some the time of the Churn. 

Life on these Floating Isles is forced to adapt or perish. Even specials that can learn to thrive in the often-vertical environments, can then be cut off from their territories and thrown into an unintended migration when the Churn comes. Flying and climbing species are moribund, and plant life has too adapted in its own ways to the chaotic environment. Some few species, however, have gained the ability to harness whatever physics defying force keeps up the land, and can instinctively manipulate this magic in many incredible ways. Creatures with this power are known to exhibit crystalline growths that pierce their hide or scales and seem to act as a focus for this magical energy.  

The effects of the Churn can be clearly seen in the human populations of the Floating Isles. Many human empires have risen to great cultural and technological heights, only to be torn apart and fall when the Churn comes. Cut off and isolated populations have diverged evolutionarily, giving rise to the modern races: short and stocky dwarves, lithe and nimble elves, elusive halflings and gnomes, now all share the world with common men and women, building their societies on the ruins of those who have come before. 

In modern times, within the northern temperate zone, there are a collection of lands and kingdoms where the races have achieved a measure of tolerance and harmony. An ancient organization known as the Ropers Guild have constructed a large network of stairways and bridges between many Isles, bringing many peoples into contact and allowing trade to flow. 

 With this trade has come not only prosperity, but also the exchange of knowledge, as great centers of learning have grown in several places. Recent advances in dirigible airships have allowed the Northern Kingdoms to expand their influence further, venturing out into the world in search of resources and treasure. Others have begun to experiment with technology derived from the magic focusing crystals taken from killed or captured beasts, and a controversial industry of hex-tech has begun to gain in provenance and in infamy. For the development of hex-tech is a dangerous endeavor, often leading to unpredictable disaster as forces are unleashed outside of an artificers’ control. 

 Our adventure truly begins on the Isle of Albony. Though not as large or powerful a kingdom as some of its neighbors, Albony is a well-connected way stop on many or the busiest trade routes in the Northern Kingdoms, and a relatively rich land due to its many vineyards and the rope vine plantations on the southern face. Here, as in many places, the call has gone out for adventurers, as tales have reached even peaceful Albony, that not all is right, and strife has found the Northern Kingdoms….. 

 

Chapter 1 – A Wind Rises 

And so, finally, we begin…. 

As the eyes of the gods looks down upon a world shrouded in mists. From the roiling core ocean below, to the highest lands above, as they move together in the steps of the great dance.  

Some of the gods slumber still, though some have prepared long for the coming days, as the dance begins its crescendo. 

The eye is drawn north to a point around which it seems the whole dance has begun to coalesce.  

As we watch, a wind rises around the peak to Cragghorn, piercing into the sunlight high above the world below. It rushes down the slopes of the mountains ever crowned in snow, and out across the great tundra across craig known as the Urg. 

Here it swirls through the orcish ruins of what was once a great tribe, now lost to history in the turning of the dance.  

To the edge of the planes the wind blows, then the down it plunges through the icey clouds as it descends the height of Cragg, the world head. It eddies and whirls around the isles that cluster at Craggs feet, gaining speed as it is sucked under a great edge and whistling between the hanging towers of overhang. Here is the songs of the dwarfs around their forges are momentarily drowned out by the winds fringed path. 

Down the road from Overhang, beneath the snow line, the wind still blew, past Dunduggen and on into the forests of albony. Almost spent now, the wind whips up the mists to the susurration or branches either side of the Mule Road. 

The mist parts and we see two figures, one small the other tall….

Distracted from his sudden urge for revenge, Tordan joins the rest of the party as they head to the Whistling Wall, the commercial district of Raveen. They manage to get two small rooms with the help of Hitch’s small rope token, and Seldom and Doro go up to bed.

Threw struggle and sewage, do DTF finally near the end of the rabbit hole? Here stands Shorty, their little rabbit, quivering in the crew’s crosshairs. His pleas and prevarications fall upon deaf ears, as Caefydd says “do you think we hang around is sewers for fun?” 

He tries distractions, appeals to authority, shade thrown at DTF and Bromtide himself, though divulges little. It seems the secrets they seek are sealed inside this serpentine chamber, and Shorty is stuck outside as well. 

He seeks escape, calling on all his skill and artifice, and if it weren’t for the potions brought by Tabatha, he may have made it leaving most of the party unconscious on the sewer floor. No, Shorty ends not with a Flourish, but in flame, mechanical spider legs melting slowly in the slime. 

Turning their attention to the grim portal, DTF set up the Cowterpult to cover the door, then Caefydd attempts to pick the lock. A click, and the door unlocks, but the iron cobras burst from the wall and attack the group. Battle is joined once more as Feindall fires the ballista at both snakes, and in response first one then the other try to encircle and grapple him, leaving him unable to dodge as a glowing crystal cobra fang plunges into his eye socket. 

As Dora dispatches the last of these Serpents, the extent of Feindall’s injury becomes clear. The eye is gone, and in its place a dark hole festering with necrotic energy. Tordan ascertains this is a kind of curse and is unable to lift it using his healing magic, so the party presses on as exhaustion begins to take its toll on Feindall. 

Iside the chamber DTF are greeted by a grizzly panorama, pipes disgorging the city's waste out an enormous hole in the floor into the morning mists below, and the now familiar grotesque machinery of their quarry. This crystal crested dark gnome part way through constructing a clockwork dragon. 

The gnome recognizes the party, trying to get them to return the eldritch weapon hilt the took from his first lair, but Tordan’s mind is strong against his Suggestion. He is revealed to be another member of the Shining Empire’s Elite magicians, allowed into the northern kingdoms as scouts to sow chaos. 

DTF are victorious in the fight, but unfortunately the gnome is killed, and Feindall’s condition worsens forcing the crew to leave the body and seeks help on the surface. Tordan takes out the blood red jewel, all that was left from the weapon hilt the gnome had, and the swirling red clouds within resolve into an eye that takes the party in before settling insistently on Feindall. Much to Tordan’s reticence, Bune takes the jewel and thrusts it into Feindalls empty eye socket, causing Feindall to finally fall unconscious. 

With the help of the shield guild, DTF get themselves, and they terrorist’s corpse, to the palace, and quarters to allow them to rest. Before they sleep, Tabatha takes her leave of the group, having finally found the revenge for her stolen glory at the wind-jammers final, which now seems so long ago..... 

Chapter 18 – New Mysteries and New Friends 

Fresh from their most fraught battle yet, DTF awake one by one in Kings Brontide’s palace in Raveen. Though now swaddled the sumptuous sheets and safety, our heroes have not escaped from their escapades unscathed. 

First to stir, Caefydd, hard at work creating a new steel defender watches his friends as each seems wracked in their slumbering struggles. Seldom must know some of his pain, as the battle that took Rex also seemed to destroy his fire spirit Tendo. He watches Feindall awaken an slowly come to terms with the loss of his eye, and the new eldritch replacement. Caefydd chuckles to himself quietly as he watches Feindall try and fail to throw a dart into one of the beams in the ceiling, obviously there may be some adjustment period to his new ominous orb. 

Caefydd hears something from the room to the right, Dora’s room. She emerges soon after have applied some strange kind of black face paint around her eyes. Soon after the dwarf comes out of the room he claimed, muttering about his lord and ravens, such a off fellow. Finally his gaze is drawn to Bune sprawled in a chair, horns silhouetted against in setting sun in the window, a glazed grin upon his face.  

Tonight has obviously brought portentous visions to this troupe, though none step forward to share any import before a knock on the door announces Wilders. He looks like a great weight has been lifted from his shoulders, as he tell the group that the unrest in the city is beginning to ebb. The shield guild have custody of the Terrorist’s second lair they discovered. His forensics people will be preparing the report for the kings and council tomorrow morning. He tells them Brontide awaits their pleasure in somewhere called the Golden Shower Lounge and Casino, and before leaving DTF to a well deserved meal, he places one final mystery on the table in the centre of the room. A small hemispherical stone, showing a sideways on laughing face with a gently glowing vir crystal eye. 

Bune is able to ascertain something of this strange stones workings. It is a communication device from the empire, and it has a pair somewhere, facilitating two way sendings. The group agree that now is not the right time to test this devise, and they follow Wilders out into the city.  

As they approach this Casino Wilders spoke of Caefydd notices a crowd has gathered, and he adds a little theatre to the troupe’s entrance and they descend the slope of the bridge flanked by syncopated blasts of piles of refuse turn to bonfires by his magic. DTF enter this Casino on a tide of applause. 

Inside, they join Brontide himself at the gaming tables. This first table is hot, and the crew make quite a return at the local dice game, as Brontide is effervescent with praise for their exploits. Eventually he invites them to play a different game in a more private space, and after sending the croupier away, he seems to drop some of his noble façade. 

Now speaking in a much rougher southern kingdoms accent, Brontide offers to become the groups patron once they reach Kingsholme. A city, he claims, is rife with intrigue and political strife. Tordan casts zone of truth and attempts to interrogate Brontide, but it seems he is earnest in what he says. The group tentatively ascent to his offer, and then while the night away with a few more drinks and games before one final day in Raveen to equip themselves and resupply before the next leg of their odyssey.... 

Chapter 19 – A Pirates Life for Me 

For the first time since meeting, DTF enjoy a small measure of comfort and leisure on their last day in Raveen, each member of the troupe spending the day alone in their personal pursuits, before they are together once more before the door of Brontide’s Counsil chamber on the morning of their departure.  

Inside they are introduced to Raveen Admiral, who will be stewarding the city while the King is away. He is fearful for his liege lord, speaking of increased dangers in the air. Even going as far as to say he no longer trusts their navigational charts, essential in the northern kingdoms’ mist shrouded skies, counselling a detour to Sundoor to procure update charts and resupply. 

Upon agreement the crew parades through the city lead by Brontide in his pomp, flanked by cheering Raveenites. Though Caefydd does notice that some of the crowd on the streets and more compelled than the clapping suggests. Upon reaching the docks, DTF can finally take in the Gladius, which proves somewhat enthusiastically named. Though armed with two ballistae and a rear mangonel, the ship is not armored heavily, looking more built for speed and capacity than war. 

The captain of the ship, a weathered gnome named Stowik, in non plussed at having to put 10 of his marines ashore to make room for Brontide and DTF, insists that they be paid a sailors’ wages and expected to do their duty while aboard. As the gladius ascends into the misty skies, the eleven Bosun Scree begins DTF’s aeronautical instruction. 

The first full day aboard ship passes without incident and the crew awake to the beautiful vista of the floating isle in sunshine. Their revery interrupted by a shout from above, the hadozee in the crow's nest has spotted something. Out of the mist rises another ship, moving somehow without visible props or sails, it sets a collision course, and once in range opens fire with its own ballistae. DTF join the crew in returning fire as the lamprey shaped craft draws closer. 

Both craft are damaged heavily as the enemy draws near enough to make out the pale faces of the gaunt crew, their eager grins of anticipation punctuated by extended fangs. Quake oh DTF for on rush a terrible scourge of the skies, a raiding party of Vampirates! The ships slam together and iron jaws on the front of the enemy clap shut as battle is joined... 

Chapter 20 – Victory over Vampires and an Unexpected Unicorn 

Splinters fly as the enemy’s ship grasps hold of the Gladius, as DTF seek to regain the initiative. Feindall leads the charge leaping aboard, green fire bursting from the blade of his glaive. He is joined by Tordan and 2REX scrambling over the Lamprey’s jaws, and moments later in a shimmer of mist, Bune stands beside them. In a storm of fire, Tendo appears amongst the vampirates, as momentum appears to be with DTF. They have taken the foredeck and blocked the pale fiends from boarding their own ship, though they cannot advance further in while remaining clipped to their safety lines. 

Feindal seems possessed by uncharacteristic aggression as his weapon slams down dispatching the first of the pirates, but the corpse explodes into ash splashing against his allies sapping their strength with necrotic energy. Bune falls, blasted by the ash and the swiping claws of the other vampirates as he attempts to move further into their ship, his body is momentarily leeched of colour before his constitution reasserts itself. The vampirates surround 2REX and Tendo, destroying both as the tables begin to turn. Two grapples blast from the enemy into the side of the Gladius and the vampirates scuttle along them like spiders on strands of web. 

Dora holds the aft deck, casting forth a roiling cloud of impenetrable darkness at the onrushing pirates, dispatching them as they flail blindly for her. Tordan rouses Bune from unconsciousness and the pair retreat as Bune electrocutes one the chains tying the ships together, hoping to dislodge the foes. Feindall leaps for other, but cannot climb across, falling to the end of his safety rope.  

One of the vampirates suddenly rises into the air and begins flinging magic about. In the heat of the battle, as he flings his own blasts of fire, Bune is overcome by the source of arcane force within him. He screams into the air and out of a blast of light from his third eye erupts a unicorn. The beast mad with rage indiscriminately attacks all around. 

One by one the pirates are dispatched, taking a few of the crew with them. Their mage calls a retreat, bringing up his own cloud of darkness for cover. Stowik and Scree subdue the unicorn as the vampirates scuttle back aboard their own ship feverishly trying to detach the chains under ranged fire from DTF. As the ships break apart, Tordan is the last left on the wrong side. He slams into the boards of the Gladius’ side, saved only by his safety line.  

With only two vampirates left aboard their vessel, yet with it somehow still making headway away from them, Caefydd makes a plea to chase them down for their loot. Stowik shoots this down and the crew exchange fire with the enemy using their ballistae and the mangonel. Just as the vampirates look to have made their escape, the final catapult shot hits a midships, splitting the lamprey in two as the pieces fall into the mists. 

Their ship gravely damaged, Brontide takes council with DTF to seek the safest path to Sundoor.... 

Chapter 21 – Any Port in a Storm 

Victory against the predations of the vampirates has cost The Gladius and her crew dearly. Two crew members are dead, and the ship severely damaged, as DTF emerge from their conference with the king. Bosun Scree waxes lyrical on DTFs heroics; and is especially inquisitive of how their more arcane feats were accomplished. Caefydd puts her off for now, as Feindall attempts to cheer the crew with a speech. 

Once the Captain emerges with the Kings, it is announced that the ship must periscope. A manoeuvre involving raising the ship above the clouds into the more dangerous open air; to gain an unobscured view of the sun, allowing for accurate positional calculations with a sextant and barometer. 

Two hours of rest is called before the periscope begins, and the grog is brough out to settle the crew’s nerves. As they drink to their fallen mates, Bune can be seen suddenly gripped with shock by the prow. He calls over those of the troupe on deck to report he received a message on the sending stone, speaking of some “Foreunner Contract” and someone called Salazaar. The group cannot decide how to respond yet, so after a bit of liquid courage, Bune attempts to imitate the Gnome terrorist and stall. 

And so, The Gladius arose into the Olympian splendour of the Sky beneath the Great Rings, and apart from Torden being smashed against the aft-castle stairs by the high winds, the ship corrected its heading and sank back into the short-lived safety of the mists. For that very evening their path is beset by an errant ice storm, something never seen this far south in living memory. DTF valiantly to their best to limit the impact, but unfortunately their effort cannot save the vessel from a forced landing on a small but densely forested Isle to initiate repairs. 

DTF leave Bune to guard the ship and set off to scout the Isle, finding evidence of a ruin town containing wall marking in Orcish script. Seldom attempts to translate, gleaning a history of strife between Gruumsh The Ruiner and Melora The Wildmother. Tordan recounts he own studies or a war between factions of the gods with Gruumsh of the losing side. After finding a small fortune amongst the ruined house, DTF progress further into an amphitheatre like area in a semi-circle around a domed building housing a tree glowing with Vir. Caefydd pulls a lever he finds hoping to open a route to the tree, which instead drops the party into a hidden room below, and the trapdoor closes above leaving the in darkness, and it doesn’t seem that they are alone....... 

 

Chapter 22 – Ooze and Arachnids 

An acrid small and harsh hissing noise fills the underground chamber, as Seldomasi is the first meet the ambling advance of the large block ooze the troupe now find themselves trapped with. Though they could not have known the reference, like the victims of the blob in 1958, DTF had been paralyzed by surprise and indecision until the puddings’ ponderous attack leaves an arc of potholes across Seldom’s leather armor. 

Dora, staying out of the enemy’s reach, lets fly with her tentacle whip, but as it’s sharp tip slashes down on the writhing gunge, it sheers straight through, and the separate halves turn towards her friends to attack independently. The situation worsens as another black pudding emerges from a grate on the opposite side of the chamber. Dora reacts instinctively, flinging Tabatha’s bladed frisbee at the new threat, as this too is cloven in twain. 

Meanwhile, unaware of this struggle, Bune is strolling nonchalantly around the deck of The Gladius, ostensibly on watch. Scree probes him about his magics, using a more flirtatious tack than before, but is rebuffed once more. As he turns away, Bune’s pocket buzzes with another sending. To Bune’s horror, his interlocutor introduces himself as Boros, the dragon-hunting, gravity-manipulating arch mages from the group’s first mission together to Mist Fall farm. Bune throws the sending stone to Scree, saying it could start her training, but the connection breaks when it leaves his hand, and Scree smashes in back in his face in her perceived betrayal. As Bune chats next to Vitruvian about context and the king, unseen behind him the forest becomes gradually yet more covered in webbing. 

Back underground, with the four slimes finally squished, the rest of DTF turn their attention from their damaged arms and armor to the trap they still stand in. After much deliberation, they decipher that the pillars represent the schools of magic; and activate a hidden door leading up to the glowing tree they had spied from the plaza outside. Seldomasi places his hand upon the trunk, and the tree retracts into a staff in his hands.  

Back on the Gladius, Bune finally notices the web now festooning the forest, as six supersized spiders swarm the ship. He fights valiantly but falls unconscious just as the rest of the party arrive to save the day.... 

 

Chapter 23 – The Harrowing of Hallelujah 

Arriving in the nick of time to thwart the arachnid attack, DTF find their friend and another crew member paralyzed. Luckily, an hour later, Bune pulls himself out of the malady with an explosive sneeze of feathers. The crew pull double shifts to get the Gladius back to flying shape, as DTF divvy up the night’s watches. 

The night passes without further incident, but for a susurration at the edge of the senses, seeming more than just the rustle of the trees. As light dawns, a wind blows past the bluff, rousing the watching Feindall from musings on a shadowy visitation he received in the night, and clearing much of the lingering mist from the woods. This reveals an enormous symbol suspended the trees, woven of webbing a wooden debris, depicting an elongated eight-pointed star. Tordan is able to identify this as the symbol of Lolth, the spider queen, another god that his order thought of as dead and gone for at least an age. 

The Gladius finally ascends above the tree-tops to restart its journey, the last sight of the atoll they had been on showing a great crack in the cliff, that from the plant growth, looks very recent. The following wind and fine weather speed the ship on its way to Sundoor for the majority of the day, until the late afternoon when the crew are shocked to see snowflakes falling in late summer. A shout from the lookout presages a cliffside town seen across the port bow through the flurries. According to Brontide’s spymaster Vertruvian, this is Hallelujah, an elven settlement famous for textiles and fashion, and he entreats the king to stop in so he can consult one of his contacts. Though as the ship draws near, there seems to be no sign of life on the snow shrouded walkways, and naught but debris in the docks. 

Vertruvian convinces the un-enthusiastic DTF to accompany him into the town, growing visibly concerned for his contact who obviously means a lot to him. The troupe move through ransacked shops and past snow-covered corpses, making their way up to the town plaza above. Arriving there they are met with a horrific edifice. A beautiful elf female, Vitruvian’s contact, is crucified in the center of the covered plaza, her left hand missing, other bodies strewn around the square with the same disfigurement. One woman looking pregnant has an ugly spike of vir crystal through her midriff, and in another room is nothing but a pile of vir pieced left hands. 

As the group reel from the macabre scene, a ghostly apparition appears, and begins to move towards then babbling about theft and the attackers of the town. She resembles the elf crucified behind them, and soon begins to rise up into the air before letting out a blood curdling scream, dropping two of the group instantly. As battle is joined with this banshee, the swarm of zombie hands and the pregnant corpse now leaking a viral miasma catch the crew in a pincer movement. DTF just scrape through this battle, and gathering what little loot there is left in the plaza, they wonder what all this horror could mean.... 

 

Chapter 24 - An Enemy from Within 

Silence falls in Hallelujah like the soft embrace of the snow. Vertruvian kneels distraught before the corpse crucified in the center of the plaza, her banshee spirit now silent too. DTF quickly and efficiently search the town before they leave, feeling some hope at the discovery of the empty aviary. Perhaps some word of this attack now flies to other kingdoms. However, a scrap of note they find implies that the attackers arrived on flying beasts; and the smoldering bonfire of books in the library and vir magic employed in the trap ominously confirm that there was more to this than just a bandit attack. 

Back aboard the ship, the troupe take council with the King, and he is not pleased. Aside from the mounting concern surrounding the attack, he admonishes the group for not reciprocating his patronage with the information they have gleaned since leaving Raveen. Accepting this criticism, DTF post watches for the night. As the ship leaves the snow, Caefydd observes amidst maudlin thoughts of death, that the storm is travelling further south. 

In the early morning light, great pillars and ridges of rock can be seen moving past on either side of the ship, their tops invisible in the clouds above. At the sound of the bell, last heard before their periscope, Caefydd enquires where they are. Gonk, eyes sunken with weariness, replies that these are the Wind Blades, marking the approach to Sundoor, the City Above the Clouds. When pressed, gonk admits he is on his second straight watch, with Scree sick in her cabin below. 

As The Gladius ascends above the clouds, the crew behold the sweeping path of the wind blades leading them toward the split mountain of Sundoor. The wind somehow sculpted to create a calm road to the sheltered valley of the city. The see tiers of buildings, docks, and fields, rising to a high tower at the very top, crested by the emblem of a sunburst. 

Feindall goes to check on the Bosun, finding her unconscious in her cramped cabin next to a pile of vomit. He picks her up to take her above deck and notices a fang of vir crystal she must have taken from the giant spiders, pushed into an incision in her arm. A green glow is spreading through her veins around it.  

Up on deck, the party fetch the king and gather round Scree as the vir starts to spread, small crystal studs breaking through the skin up along her arm. Bune can only sense chaos in her mind, so Caefydd goes for a rope to bind her to the deck. As he grabs her, a burst of lightning blasts out scroching the party, they attempt to engage as she conjures a wall of ice trapping half of them on the wrong side. As they try to bring her down, her body is gradually taken over by the vir, arcane energy lashing out mindlessly in all directions, killing several of the crew. She alone is a match for them, dropping member after member of DTF, as they struggle to heal quickly enough to stay in the fight. Caefydd is finally able to strike the killing blow, just as a great cracking sound rends the air....
