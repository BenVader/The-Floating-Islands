THE FLOATING ISLES CHAPTER SYNOPSIS 

Prologue  

Our story is set in the world of The Floating Isles, where mysterious forces have raised up the land, and hold it suspended in the air. From small archipelagos of little more than rocks and boulders, to majestic continental shelves; these lands hang at different heights above the world ocean, that broils deep below with storms and mist.  

These Floating Isles for the most part move glacially slowly, in an intricate dance that when measured at the human level can be barely perceptible, punctuated only by a few rogue islands that soar across the sky to their own inscrutable beat. Legends speak of a cycle of periodical chaos, where the sedate steps are interrupted by unpredictable upheaval, when even the largest and most populated continents are not safe from quakes and collisions. The periods are called by some the time of the Churn. 

Life on these Floating Isles is forced to adapt or perish. Even specials that can learn to thrive in the often-vertical environments, can then be cut off from their territories and thrown into an unintended migration when the Churn comes. Flying and climbing species are moribund, and plant life has too adapted in its own ways to the chaotic environment. Some few species, however, have gained the ability to harness whatever physics defying force keeps up the land, and can instinctively manipulate this magic in many incredible ways. Creatures with this power are known to exhibit crystalline growths that pierce their hide or scales and seem to act as a focus for this magical energy.  

The effects of the Churn can be clearly seen in the human populations of the Floating Isles. Many human empires have risen to great cultural and technological heights, only to be torn apart and fall when the Churn comes. Cut off and isolated populations have diverged evolutionarily, giving rise to the modern races: short and stocky dwarves, lithe and nimble elves, elusive halflings and gnomes, now all share the world with common men and women, building their societies on the ruins of those who have come before. 

In modern times, within the northern temperate zone, there are a collection of lands and kingdoms where the races have achieved a measure of tolerance and harmony. An ancient organization known as the Ropers Guild have constructed a large network of stairways and bridges between many Isles, bringing many peoples into contact and allowing trade to flow. 

 With this trade has come not only prosperity, but also the exchange of knowledge, as great centers of learning have grown in several places. Recent advances in dirigible airships have allowed the Northern Kingdoms to expand their influence further, venturing out into the world in search of resources and treasure. Others have begun to experiment with technology derived from the magic focusing crystals taken from killed or captured beasts, and a controversial industry of hex-tech has begun to gain in provenance and in infamy. For the development of hex-tech is a dangerous endeavor, often leading to unpredictable disaster as forces are unleashed outside of an artificers’ control. 

 Our adventure truly begins on the Isle of Albony. Though not as large or powerful a kingdom as some of its neighbors, Albony is a well-connected way stop on many or the busiest trade routes in the Northern Kingdoms, and a relatively rich land due to its many vineyards and the rope vine plantations on the southern face. Here, as in many places, the call has gone out for adventurers, as tales have reached even peaceful Albony, that not all is right, and strife has found the Northern Kingdoms….. 

 

Chapter 1 – A Wind Rises 

And so, finally, we begin…. 

As the eyes of the gods looks down upon a world shrouded in mists. From the roiling core ocean below, to the highest lands above, as they move together in the steps of the great dance.  

Some of the gods slumber still, though some have prepared long for the coming days, as the dance begins its crescendo. 

The eye is drawn north to a point around which it seems the whole dance has begun to coalesce.  

As we watch, a wind rises around the peak to Cragghorn, piercing into the sunlight high above the world below. It rushes down the slopes of the mountains ever crowned in snow, and out across the great tundra across craig known as the Urg. 

Here it swirls through the orcish ruins of what was once a great tribe, now lost to history in the turning of the dance.  

To the edge of the planes the wind blows, then the down it plunges through the icey clouds as it descends the height of Cragg, the world head. It eddies and whirls around the isles that cluster at Craggs feet, gaining speed as it is sucked under a great edge and whistling between the hanging towers of overhang. Here is the songs of the dwarfs around their forges are momentarily drowned out by the winds fringed path. 

Down the road from Overhang, beneath the snow line, the wind still blew, past Dunduggen and on into the forests of albony. Almost spent now, the wind whips up the mists to the susurration or branches either side of the Mule Road. 

The mist parts and we see two figures, one small the other tall….

Distracted from his sudden urge for revenge, Tordan joins the rest of the party as they head to the Whistling Wall, the commercial district of Raveen. They manage to get two small rooms with the help of Hitch’s small rope token, and Seldom and Doro go up to bed.

Threw struggle and sewage, do DTF finally near the end of the rabbit hole? Here stands Shorty, their little rabbit, quivering in the crew’s crosshairs. His pleas and prevarications fall upon deaf ears, as Caefydd says “do you think we hang around is sewers for fun?” 

He tries distractions, appeals to authority, shade thrown at DTF and Bromtide himself, though divulges little. It seems the secrets they seek are sealed inside this serpentine chamber, and Shorty is stuck outside as well. 

He seeks escape, calling on all his skill and artifice, and if it weren’t for the potions brought by Tabatha, he may have made it leaving most of the party unconscious on the sewer floor. No, Shorty ends not with a Flourish, but in flame, mechanical spider legs melting slowly in the slime. 

Turning their attention to the grim portal, DTF set up the Cowterpult to cover the door, then Caefydd attempts to pick the lock. A click, and the door unlocks, but the iron cobras burst from the wall and attack the group. Battle is joined once more as Feindall fires the ballista at both snakes, and in response first one then the other try to encircle and grapple him, leaving him unable to dodge as a glowing crystal cobra fang plunges into his eye socket. 

As Dora dispatches the last of these Serpents, the extent of Feindall’s injury becomes clear. The eye is gone, and in its place a dark hole festering with necrotic energy. Tordan ascertains this is a kind of curse and is unable to lift it using his healing magic, so the party presses on as exhaustion begins to take its toll on Feindall. 

Iside the chamber DTF are greeted by a grizzly panorama, pipes disgorging the city's waste out an enormous hole in the floor into the morning mists below, and the now familiar grotesque machinery of their quarry. This crystal crested dark gnome part way through constructing a clockwork dragon. 

The gnome recognizes the party, trying to get them to return the eldritch weapon hilt the took from his first lair, but Tordan’s mind is strong against his Suggestion. He is revealed to be another member of the Shining Empire’s Elite magicians, allowed into the northern kingdoms as scouts to sow chaos. 

DTF are victorious in the fight, but unfortunately the gnome is killed, and Feindall’s condition worsens forcing the crew to leave the body and seeks help on the surface. Tordan takes out the blood red jewel, all that was left from the weapon hilt the gnome had, and the swirling red clouds within resolve into an eye that takes the party in before settling insistently on Feindall. Much to Tordan’s reticence, Bune takes the jewel and thrusts it into Feindalls empty eye socket, causing Feindall to finally fall unconscious. 

With the help of the shield guild, DTF get themselves, and they terrorist’s corpse, to the palace, and quarters to allow them to rest. Before they sleep, Tabatha takes her leave of the group, having finally found the revenge for her stolen glory at the wind-jammers final, which now seems so long ago..... 

Chapter 18 – New Mysteries and New Friends 

Fresh from their most fraught battle yet, DTF awake one by one in Kings Brontide’s palace in Raveen. Though now swaddled the sumptuous sheets and safety, our heroes have not escaped from their escapades unscathed. 

First to stir, Caefydd, hard at work creating a new steel defender watches his friends as each seems wracked in their slumbering struggles. Seldom must know some of his pain, as the battle that took Rex also seemed to destroy his fire spirit Tendo. He watches Feindall awaken an slowly come to terms with the loss of his eye, and the new eldritch replacement. Caefydd chuckles to himself quietly as he watches Feindall try and fail to throw a dart into one of the beams in the ceiling, obviously there may be some adjustment period to his new ominous orb. 

Caefydd hears something from the room to the right, Dora’s room. She emerges soon after have applied some strange kind of black face paint around her eyes. Soon after the dwarf comes out of the room he claimed, muttering about his lord and ravens, such a off fellow. Finally his gaze is drawn to Bune sprawled in a chair, horns silhouetted against in setting sun in the window, a glazed grin upon his face.  

Tonight has obviously brought portentous visions to this troupe, though none step forward to share any import before a knock on the door announces Wilders. He looks like a great weight has been lifted from his shoulders, as he tell the group that the unrest in the city is beginning to ebb. The shield guild have custody of the Terrorist’s second lair they discovered. His forensics people will be preparing the report for the kings and council tomorrow morning. He tells them Brontide awaits their pleasure in somewhere called the Golden Shower Lounge and Casino, and before leaving DTF to a well deserved meal, he places one final mystery on the table in the centre of the room. A small hemispherical stone, showing a sideways on laughing face with a gently glowing vir crystal eye. 

Bune is able to ascertain something of this strange stones workings. It is a communication device from the empire, and it has a pair somewhere, facilitating two way sendings. The group agree that now is not the right time to test this devise, and they follow Wilders out into the city.  

As they approach this Casino Wilders spoke of Caefydd notices a crowd has gathered, and he adds a little theatre to the troupe’s entrance and they descend the slope of the bridge flanked by syncopated blasts of piles of refuse turn to bonfires by his magic. DTF enter this Casino on a tide of applause. 

Inside, they join Brontide himself at the gaming tables. This first table is hot, and the crew make quite a return at the local dice game, as Brontide is effervescent with praise for their exploits. Eventually he invites them to play a different game in a more private space, and after sending the croupier away, he seems to drop some of his noble façade. 

Now speaking in a much rougher southern kingdoms accent, Brontide offers to become the groups patron once they reach Kingsholme. A city, he claims, is rife with intrigue and political strife. Tordan casts zone of truth and attempts to interrogate Brontide, but it seems he is earnest in what he says. The group tentatively ascent to his offer, and then while the night away with a few more drinks and games before one final day in Raveen to equip themselves and resupply before the next leg of their odyssey....
